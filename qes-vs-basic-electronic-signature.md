# QES vs Basic Electronic Signature: What's the Difference?

Electronic signatures are widely used in modern business processes, but not all electronic signatures provide the same level of trust and legal validity.

## Basic Electronic Signature

A basic electronic signature may include:

- Typed names in emails
- Scanned handwritten signatures
- Click-to-sign actions
- Simple electronic approvals

These methods can be suitable for low-risk workflows but may not provide strong identity verification.

## Qualified Electronic Signature (QES)

A Qualified Electronic Signature (QES) uses:

- Verified digital certificates
- Secure cryptographic keys
- Trusted service providers
- Strong identity verification

QES provides a significantly higher level of assurance regarding the signer's identity.

## Why Organizations Use QES

Organizations often adopt QES to:

- Protect sensitive business documents
- Improve regulatory compliance
- Reduce fraud risks
- Support secure digital workflows

## Security Considerations

When implementing QES:

- Protect signature tokens
- Monitor certificate expiration
- Restrict signing permissions
- Maintain secure backup procedures

## Conclusion

QES helps organizations establish stronger trust in digital transactions while improving security and efficiency.
